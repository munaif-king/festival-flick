12 Islamic Months

Sr Arabic Name Transliteration Meaning / Note
1 مُحَرَّم Muharram Sacred month (one of the 4 sacred months)
2 صَفَر Safar Means "void" or "empty"
3 رَبِيع ٱلْأَوَّل Rabi' al-Awwal "First spring" – Birth month of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ
4 رَبِيع ٱلثَّانِي Rabi' al-Thani "Second spring" (Also called Rabi' al-Akhir)
5 جُمَادَىٰ ٱلْأُولَىٰ Jumada al-Awwal "First dry" (winter/dry month)
6 جُمَادَىٰ ٱلثَّانِيَة Jumada al-Thani "Second dry" (Also called Jumada al-Akhirah)
7 رَجَب Rajab Sacred month
8 شَعْبَان Sha'ban Month before Ramadan
9 رَمَضَان Ramadan Month of fasting (most sacred)
10 شَوَّال Shawwal Eid al-Fitr is on 1st Shawwal
11 ذُو ٱلْقَعْدَة Dhul-Qa'dah Sacred month, time of peace
12 ذُو ٱلْحِجَّة Dhul-Hijjah Hajj and Eid al-Adha take place

Notes:

  • 4 sacred months in Islam: Muharram, Rajab, Dhul-Qa'dah, Dhul-Hijjah.
  • Ramadan is the most spiritually significant month — fasting (sawm) is observed.
  • Dhul-Hijjah is when Hajj takes place.

Islamic Festivals

Ashura عاشوراء

For Sunni Muslims: A day of optional fasting to remember Prophet Musa (Moses) and his people’s escape from Pharaoh. For Shia Muslims: A major day of mourning to honor the martyrdom of Imam Hussain (RA) at Karbala. Processions, remembrance, and fasting are observed.

Rabi' al-Awwal رَبِيع ٱلْأَوَّل

Prophet Muhammad’s Birthday When: 12th Rabi' al-Awwal What it is: Celebration of the birth of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ. Traditions: Naat (poetry), Seerah lectures, processions, charity. Celebrated by many Muslims; others consider it non-mandatory.

Shab-e-Barat لیلۃ البراءۃ

When: Night of 15th Sha'ban What it is: A night for seeking forgiveness and reflecting on one’s deeds. Traditions: Prayers, reciting the Qur'an, visiting graves (in some cultures), fasting the next day. Note: Its significance is debated among scholars.

Ramadan رَمَضَان

Ninth month of the Islamic (Hijri) calendar. It is the holiest and most spiritual month for Muslims. Mentioned in the Qur’an as the month in which the Qur’an was revealed.

Eid al-Fitr عید الفطر

Festival of Breaking the Fast When: 1st of Shawwal (after Ramadan ends) What it celebrates: Marks the end of the holy month of fasting (Ramadan). A day of celebration, prayer, charity (Zakat al-Fitr), and community gatherings. Traditions: Eid prayer, wearing new clothes, visiting family/friends, sharing sweets and food.

Explore Discover Gallery

Eid al-Adha عید الأضحى

Festival of Sacrifice When: 10th of Dhul-Hijjah (during Hajj) What it commemorates: Honors Prophet Ibrahim’s (Abraham’s) willingness to sacrifice his son for Allah. Traditions: Eid prayer, animal sacrifice (Qurbani), distributing meat to the poor, family meals. Also known as: Bakrid or the Greater Eid.

Festival FAQs

Ashura, on the 10th of Muharram, remembers Imam Hussain’s sacrifice. It’s a day for reflection, prayer, and coming together as a community to honor courage and compassion.

Ramadan is a month of fasting, prayer, and gratitude. It’s a chance to grow spiritually, help others, and celebrate together at Eid al-Fitr.

Mawlid al-Nabi celebrates the Prophet Muhammad’s birth with prayers, stories, and shared meals. Communities gather to reflect on his life and spread kindness.
