Festival FAQs

Diwali, also known as the Festival of Lights, symbolizes the victory of light over darkness and good over evil. It marks the return of Lord Rama to Ayodhya after defeating Ravana and also honors Goddess Lakshmi, the goddess of wealth and prosperity.

Holi celebrates the arrival of spring and the triumph of good over evil. Throwing colors symbolizes joy, unity, and the breaking down of social barriers. It is also linked to the playful acts of Lord Krishna with Radha and the gopis.

Navratri is a nine-night festival dedicated to the worship of Goddess Durga in her various forms. It is celebrated with fasting, prayers, traditional dances like Garba and Dandiya, and devotional music. Each day is associated with a different form of the goddess.
